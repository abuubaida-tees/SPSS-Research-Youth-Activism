## üìâ Statistical Analysis (SPSS Results)

### **1. Hypothesis Testing (Regression Model)**
*Attempting to predict political movement participation based on social media frequency.*

| Variable | Coefficient (B) | t-Value | Sig. (p) | Result |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Constant** | 1.280 | 10.867 | .000 | Significant |
| **Social Media Freq.** | -.007 | -.142 | .887 | **Null Hypothesis Accepted** |
*> Insight: Frequency of use does NOT automatically translate to campaign participation.*

### **2. Pearson Correlation Matrix**
*Testing relationships between different online behaviors.*

| Behavior A | Behavior B | Pearson (R) | Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :---: | :---: |
| Shared Political Content | Participated in Online Petition | **0.207** | **0.002 (Significant)** |
| Platform Choice | Raising Awareness | 0.072 | .284 (Not Sig) |

## üîé Key Demographic Insights
*Analysis of N=223 Respondents*

* **86.1%** reported being harassed/targeted for their political views online.
* **79.8%** have participated in a specific online political campaign.
* **57.0%** use social media for political purposes on a **daily basis**.
